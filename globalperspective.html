Global Perspective

The digital divide significantly affects all kinds of people around the world. Here are some demographics that are largely influenced:

Low-Income Families: Affordability is the most significant barrier, affecting access to both devices and internet services.

Rural and Remote Communities: Lack of broadband infrastructure makes it difficult to access essential services like education and healthcare.

Elderly: Many face digital exclusion due to a lack of digital literacy, physical limitations, and limited access to technology.

Minority and Marginalized Communities: Socio-economic and systemic barriers contribute to digital exclusion, though some access is available through community programs.

People with Disabilities: Accessibility remains a concern despite technological advancements, with significant barriers in website accessibility and device availability.
